# Arena V6 Combat Clarity

Arena V6 is a presentation and UX layer over the existing Arena Clean v48 client and BE2 contracts. It does not replace the backend, Socket.IO events or the `arena_command_v1` flow.

## What Changed

- Premium fantasy neon visual layer for the battlefield, lanes, HUD, cards and timeline.
- Clear step guidance: strategy, card, lane/target, ready and resolution.
- Cards now expose Creature, Spell and Trap intent visually.
- Spells highlight valid or inferred targets and send a compatible backend target contract.
- Traps use a visible Trap Zone and a "prepare" action before Ready.
- Timeline events are normalized into readable combat beats.
- First battle tutorial explains the flow inside Training without blocking gameplay.
- Card art fallbacks use the card art manifest when no final image exists.

## Battle Flow

1. Choose Strategy: Strike pressures, Guard protects, Focus builds Ambition.
2. Play a Card: choose one playable card or skip with Ready.
3. Creature: choose an empty lane.
4. Spell: choose a highlighted target or cast now when the effect is self/global.
5. Trap: prepare it into the Trap Zone.
6. Ready: lock the round and watch the timeline resolve.

## Creature

Creature cards enter empty lanes and fight during round resolution. If lanes are full, the UI explains that the player should use a Spell, prepare a Trap or press Ready.

## Spell

Spell target UX is defensive and now backed by a minimum BE2 resolver:

- Damage-like spell text highlights enemy targets and the enemy hero.
- Shield/heal-like spell text highlights allied targets and self.
- Draw/Ambition/Focus-like spell text casts on self.
- Global or unclear spells can cast now.

The frontend still sends a legacy-compatible target token, but it also sends `target_type`, `target_owner`, `target_lane`, `cast_mode` and `client_selected_target` so BE2 can resolve damage, shield, heal or Ambition effects when possible.

## Trap

Trap cards are presented as prepared cards in the Trap Zone. BE2 now exposes prepared traps through canonical field payloads when available, while Arena V6 keeps a local fallback for old or incomplete states.

## UX Decisions

- The Ready button is still accessible but no longer dominates the entire screen.
- The hand remains backend-canonical; no card is removed visually unless a canonical state confirms it.
- Target highlights never block the player permanently. Selecting a Spell always has a fallback action.
- Reduced motion is respected by disabling new transitions and animation emphasis.

## Known Limits

- Spell/Trap resolution is V1 and intentionally conservative; advanced chained effects and card draw are not expanded yet.
- Trap trigger text comes from backend events when available and remains defensively inferred for legacy payloads.
- Placeholder card art is generated by CSS/manifest data until final image files are produced.

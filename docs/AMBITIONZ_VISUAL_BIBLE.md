# Ambitionz Visual Bible

Ambitionz uses an original dark fantasy premium direction: tactical magic duels on ancient stone, aged metal card frames, parchment-like information surfaces and restrained magical light. The product should feel like a collectible fantasy TCG, not a generic neon dashboard or SaaS template.

## Core Direction

- Dark fantasy premium: deep shadow, warm metal, worn stone, parchment and focused magic light.
- Tactical magic arena: the battlefield is the main character; lanes must read before decoration.
- Ancient stone board: lane tiles feel carved, weighty and readable.
- Aged metal frames: cards, hero plates and command buttons use bronze, iron and gold accents.
- Parchment panels: logs, summaries and teaching copy use readable parchment surfaces.
- Magical lane glow: valid lanes and combat feedback glow briefly, with purpose.
- Restrained particles/glow: light is a gameplay signal, not a background gimmick.
- Fallback-first: every card must look intentional even without final art.

## Anti-Patterns

- No generic neon web UI as the primary identity.
- No flat SaaS cards, dashboard grids or marketing-card panels inside the Arena.
- No copying layouts, UI marks, icons, names, assets or proprietary identity from existing games.
- No tiny text over bright gradients without a readable backing surface.
- No permanent panel pile-up that competes with the battlefield.
- No decorative glow that obscures lanes, hand cards or action buttons.

## Arena System

The Arena is built around five readable zones:

1. Compact enemy hero plate.
2. Enemy lane row.
3. Ancient stone battlefield divider.
4. Player lane row.
5. Player command dock with hero status, hand and actions.

The side chronicle can teach, summarize and show logs, but it must never become the visual center. On mobile, the hand becomes a horizontal dock and actions remain reachable without covering cards.

## Card Frame System

Cards use a fantasy TCG frame with:

- Metal outer frame.
- Art window or premium fallback art.
- Clear nameplate.
- Energy/cost medallion.
- Type badge for Creature, Spell or Trap.
- Role badge for tactical teaching.
- ATK/HP for creatures or VAL/element for non-creatures.
- Rarity accent that supports readability.

Type identity:

- Creature: bronze/gold frame, lane presence, creature/stat clarity.
- Spell: violet/silver arcane frame, target/cast clarity.
- Trap: amber/iron sealed frame, prepare/trigger clarity.

## Element Palettes

- Fire: dark ember, copper, lava orange, gold sparks; pressure and direct damage.
- Water: deep blue stone, cyan reflection, silver rune light; focus and sustain.
- Earth: moss stone, bronze armor, muted green; defense and durable bodies.
- Plant: root green, living vine light, amber sap; control and growth.
- Global/Neutral: smoky violet, tarnished silver, old arcane parchment; flexible utility.

## UI Components

- Hero plates: compact aged-metal plaques with HP/EN/AMB badges.
- Buttons: command plates, not web buttons; selected/disabled states must be visible.
- Logs and tutorial: parchment/chronicle surfaces with short copy.
- Empty lane: carved stone seal with subtle dashed rune, not a blank box.
- Active lane: focused glow with border, not a flood of color.
- Combat feedback: damage pulse, ward pulse, spell impact and trap reveal are brief and reduced-motion safe.

## Mobile Rules

- The battlefield remains visible before ornamental panels.
- Hand cards must keep readable cost, name, type and role.
- Action buttons cannot cover the hand.
- Tutorial overlays must be compact and dismissible.
- Lanes may scroll horizontally only when necessary, and must remain tappable.

## Copy Tone

Use direct, fantasy-flavored guidance:

- "Choose a strategy."
- "Summon into a lane."
- "Cast on a valid target."
- "Prepare this trap."
- "Press Ready to resolve."

Avoid technical payload language, raw JSON, backend terms and fake feature promises.
